The Titans

In Greek mythology, the Titans weere any of the offspring of Uranus (representing Heaven) and Gaea (representing Earth) and their respective descendants. In the beginning, there were believed to have been 12 original Titans: Oceanus, Coeus, Crius, Hyperion, Iapetus, Cronus, Thea, rhea, Themis, Mnemosyne, Pheobe, and Tethys. The Titans rebelled against their ruling father, managing to overthrow Uranus and establishing Cronus as their ruler. In an ironic twist, Cronus' own son Zeus would rebel against the Titans and succeed himself, casting all the Titans into the depths of Tarturus, the underworld. Zeus and his bretheren would then rule Olympus. Prometheus and Epithemeus, though Titans, swore loyalty to Zeus because they knew of a prophecy decreeing Zeus' eventual victory; rather than become enemies, they assisted the god instead.
